PRESIDIO HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY MANUAL
Company: Presidio
Effective Date: January 20, 2026
Last Revised: January 20, 2026
Policy Version: 1.0
Section 1: Employment Practices and Equal Opportunity
Presidio is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local laws. All employment decisions, including recruitment, hiring, compensation, promotion, training, discipline, and termination, shall be made based solely on merit, qualifications, and business needs. Presidio maintains a zero-tolerance policy for discrimination and harassment of any kind. Any employee who believes they have been subjected to discrimination or harassment should immediately report the incident to their supervisor, the Human Resources Department, or through the company's confidential reporting hotline. All complaints will be promptly investigated, and appropriate corrective action will be taken when violations are substantiated.
Section 2: Compensation and Benefits
Presidio offers a competitive compensation package designed to attract and retain talented employees. All employees are classified as either exempt or non-exempt under the Fair Labor Standards Act, and compensation is determined based on job responsibilities, market rates, individual performance, and internal equity considerations. Salaries are reviewed annually, and adjustments may be made based on performance evaluations, market conditions, and budget availability. Presidio provides a comprehensive benefits package including health insurance, dental and vision coverage, life insurance, disability insurance, retirement savings plans with employer matching contributions, paid time off, and various voluntary benefits. Employees become eligible for benefits on the first day of the month following sixty days of continuous employment. Detailed information regarding specific benefit plans, eligibility requirements, enrollment periods, and coverage levels is available through the Human Resources Department.
Section 3: Working Hours, Attendance, and Time Off
The standard workweek at Presidio consists of forty hours, typically Monday through Friday, although specific schedules may vary by department and position. Non-exempt employees are required to accurately record all hours worked using the company's timekeeping system, and any overtime must be pre-approved by management. Presidio recognizes the importance of work-life balance and offers various types of leave including vacation time, sick leave, personal days, and holidays. Full-time employees accrue fifteen days of paid vacation annually during their first three years of employment, with accrual rates increasing with tenure. Employees also receive ten paid holidays per year and accrue one day of sick leave per month. Presidio complies with all applicable federal and state leave laws, including the Family and Medical Leave Act, providing eligible employees with up to twelve weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave for qualifying reasons.
Section 4: Code of Conduct and Professional Standards
All Presidio employees are expected to conduct themselves in a professional, ethical, and respectful manner at all times. Employees must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and company policies, and must act with integrity in all business dealings. This includes maintaining confidentiality of proprietary and sensitive information, avoiding conflicts of interest, refusing to engage in bribery or corruption, and accurately reporting financial information and business transactions. Employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, vendors, and all business contacts with respect and courtesy, fostering a collaborative and inclusive work environment. Violation of the code of conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. Employees who observe or suspect violations of law, policy, or ethical standards are encouraged to report such concerns without fear of retaliation through appropriate channels including their supervisor or the Human Resources Department.
Section 5: Performance Management and Employee Development
Presidio is committed to supporting employee growth and development through regular performance feedback, training opportunities, and career advancement programs. All employees participate in an annual performance review process during which job performance, goal achievement, competencies, and development needs are evaluated. Supervisors and employees work together to establish clear performance objectives aligned with departmental and organizational goals. Presidio encourages continuous learning and provides access to various development resources including internal training programs, external workshops and conferences, online learning platforms, mentoring relationships, and tuition reimbursement for approved courses related to current or future roles within the organization. Employees demonstrating strong performance and leadership potential may be considered for advancement opportunities as positions become available. Performance improvement plans may be implemented when employee performance falls below acceptable standards, providing structured support and clear expectations for improvement.
Section 6: Workplace Safety and Health
Presidio prioritizes the health, safety, and well-being of all employees and maintains a comprehensive workplace safety program in compliance with Occupational Safety and Health Administration regulations and applicable state and local requirements. The company provides a safe working environment through regular safety inspections, hazard assessments, appropriate safety equipment and protective gear, emergency preparedness plans, and ongoing safety training. All employees are responsible for following safety protocols, reporting unsafe conditions or practices, participating in required safety training, and using personal protective equipment as directed. Workplace accidents or injuries must be reported immediately to a supervisor and the Human Resources Department. Presidio prohibits working under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs and maintains a drug-free workplace policy. The company may conduct drug and alcohol testing in accordance with applicable laws, including pre-employment screening, reasonable suspicion testing, post-accident testing, and random testing for safety-sensitive positions.
Section 7: Confidentiality and Intellectual Property
Employees of Presidio have access to confidential and proprietary information that is critical to the company's competitive advantage and business success. All employees must sign confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements as a condition of employment and must safeguard sensitive information including trade secrets, client lists, financial data, strategic plans, product development information, and any other proprietary materials. Confidential information may not be disclosed to unauthorized parties during or after employment without proper authorization. All work products, inventions, discoveries, improvements, and creative works developed by employees within the scope of their employment or using company resources are the sole property of Presidio. Employees must promptly disclose all such developments to the company and assign all rights, titles, and interests to Presidio. Upon termination of employment, employees must return all company property including documents, data, equipment, and access credentials, and must not retain copies of any confidential or proprietary information.
Section 8: Technology Use and Social Media
Presidio provides technology resources including computers, mobile devices, email systems, internet access, and software applications to facilitate employee productivity and business operations. These resources are company property and must be used primarily for legitimate business purposes in accordance with acceptable use policies. The company reserves the right to monitor, access, and review employee use of technology resources to ensure compliance with policies, investigate misconduct, and protect company interests. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company technology systems. Personal use of technology resources should be minimal, appropriate, and must not interfere with job responsibilities or violate any company policies. Regarding social media, employees are free to maintain personal social media accounts but must exercise good judgment when posting content that could reflect on Presidio. Employees may not disclose confidential company information, make disparaging comments about the company or colleagues, or represent personal opinions as official company positions on social media platforms.
Section 9: Disciplinary Procedures and Termination
Presidio maintains progressive disciplinary procedures designed to address performance deficiencies and policy violations while providing employees with opportunities to improve. Depending on the nature and severity of the issue, disciplinary actions may include verbal warnings, written warnings, performance improvement plans, suspension, demotion, or termination of employment. Certain serious violations including theft, violence, harassment, discrimination, fraud, gross insubordination, or violation of safety rules may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline. Employment with Presidio is at-will, meaning either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice, except where prohibited by law or contract. Upon separation from employment, departing employees must participate in an exit process including return of company property, completion of exit interviews, final paycheck processing in accordance with applicable wage laws, and information regarding continuation of benefits under COBRA where applicable.
Section 10: Policy Administration and Amendments
This Human Resources Policy Manual is maintained by the Presidio Human Resources Department and applies to all employees of the company. The policies contained herein are intended to provide guidance and establish standards for employment practices, but do not constitute a contract of employment or guarantee of continued employment. Presidio reserves the right to modify, amend, suspend, or terminate any policy or benefit at any time with or without notice, as necessary to meet business needs, comply with legal requirements, or address changing circumstances. Questions regarding interpretation or application of these policies should be directed to the Human Resources Department. Employees are responsible for reading, understanding, and complying with all policies outlined in this manual and any subsequent updates. Acknowledgment of receipt and understanding of this policy manual will be documented in each employee's personnel file. Policy updates will be communicated through official company channels, and employees will be required to acknowledge receipt of significant policy changes affecting their employment.
