PRESIDIO IT POLICY AND PROCEDURES MANUAL

1. VPN Access and Usage Policy
All employees must use the company-approved Virtual Private Network (VPN) to access internal systems when working remotely. VPN access ensures secure communication between user devices and internal company resources. Unauthorized VPN software is strictly prohibited. Users must authenticate using their corporate credentials and multi-factor authentication (MFA) where applicable.
2. VPN Setup Procedure
To set up the company VPN, employees must download the approved VPN client from the internal software portal. Supported operating systems include Windows 10 or later and macOS 12 or later. After installation, users should log in using their corporate email and password. Detailed setup guides are available in the "VPN Setup Guide" document.
3. Approved Software List
Only software approved by the IT department may be installed on company-owned devices. Approved software includes Microsoft Office Suite, Google Chrome, Visual Studio Code, Slack, Zoom, and company-approved antivirus tools. Installation of unapproved software may result in security risks and disciplinary action.
4. Restricted and Prohibited Software
The installation or use of peer-to-peer file sharing software, unauthorized VPN clients, cracked software, and personal cloud storage tools is prohibited. These applications pose security, compliance, and data leakage risks. Requests for exceptions must be submitted to the IT Security team for review.
5. Laptop and Device Request Process
Employees requiring a new laptop or replacement device must submit a request through the internal IT Service Portal. Requests must include the reason for the request, required specifications, and manager approval. Standard issue laptops are provided based on role and availability.
6. Hardware Replacement and Repair
In the event of hardware malfunction or damage, employees must report the issue immediately via the IT Service Portal. IT will assess whether the device can be repaired or requires replacement. Personal repairs or third-party servicing of company devices is not permitted.
7. IT Security and Password Policy
All users must adhere to the company's password policy, which requires passwords to be at least 12 characters long and changed every 90 days. Passwords must not be shared with others. MFA is mandatory for accessing sensitive systems, including VPN and internal dashboards.
8. Email and Communication Tools
Company email and approved communication tools must be used for all official work-related communication. Employees should be cautious of phishing emails and report suspicious messages to the IT Security team immediately. External forwarding of company emails is not allowed without prior approval.
9. Data Storage and Backup Guidelines
Work-related files must be stored only in company-approved storage systems such as the internal file server or approved cloud storage platforms. Local storage on personal devices is discouraged. Automated backups are performed regularly to prevent data loss.
10. IT Support and Issue Reporting
For any IT-related issues, employees should raise a ticket via the IT Service Portal. Tickets should include a clear description of the problem, screenshots if applicable, and urgency level. The IT support team operates during standard business hours and follows defined service-level agreements (SLAs).
11. Remote Work Technology Requirements
Employees working remotely must ensure they have a stable internet connection with minimum speeds of 25 Mbps download and 10 Mbps upload. A dedicated workspace with proper lighting for video calls is recommended. Presidio provides stipends for home office equipment including monitors, keyboards, ergonomic chairs, and headsets upon manager approval. Remote workers must ensure their home network is secured with WPA3 encryption and a strong password.
12. Mobile Device Management (MDM) Policy
All company-issued mobile devices (smartphones and tablets) must be enrolled in Presidio's Mobile Device Management system within 24 hours of receipt. MDM enables remote security features including device encryption, remote wipe capability, and application management. Personal devices used for work purposes (BYOD) must also be enrolled if accessing company email or confidential data. Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT Security immediately for remote locking and data protection.
13. Acceptable Use of Company Technology Resources
Presidio technology resources are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted during breaks provided it does not interfere with work duties or violate company policies. Prohibited activities include: accessing inappropriate content, streaming non-work-related media excessively, using company resources for personal business ventures, downloading pirated content, or engaging in activities that could damage Presidio's reputation. Violation of acceptable use policies may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
14. Cloud Services and SaaS Application Policy
All cloud services and Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) applications must be approved by IT before use. Approved cloud platforms include Microsoft Azure, AWS (for authorized projects), Google Workspace, Salesforce, and ServiceNow. Shadow IT—the use of unapproved cloud services—poses significant security and compliance risks. Employees who identify a business need for a new cloud service should submit a request through the IT Service Portal including business justification, vendor information, and data classification details.
15. Data Classification and Handling Standards
Presidio classifies data into four categories: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted. Public data can be freely shared. Internal data is for employee use only. Confidential data includes customer information, financial records, and strategic plans requiring encryption at rest and in transit. Restricted data includes personally identifiable information (PII), protected health information (PHI), and payment card data requiring the highest level of security controls. Employees must label documents appropriately and follow handling procedures based on classification level.
16. Incident Response and Cybersecurity Protocol
Any suspected security incident—including phishing attempts, malware detection, unauthorized access, data breaches, or lost devices—must be reported to the IT Security team immediately via email (security@presidio.com) or by calling the security hotline at ext. 9911. Do not attempt to investigate or remediate security incidents independently. The Security Operations Center (SOC) operates 24/7 and will coordinate incident response. Employees must preserve evidence and cooperate fully with investigations. Timely reporting is critical and will not result in disciplinary action when done in good faith.
17. Software License Compliance and Asset Management
Presidio is committed to full compliance with software licensing agreements. All software installations are tracked through the IT Asset Management system. Employees must not share licenses, install software on multiple devices beyond license terms, or use personal licenses for company work. Annual software audits are conducted to ensure compliance. The Software Asset Management team maintains a catalog of all licensed software and tracks usage to optimize costs and prevent violations.
18. Network Access Control and Guest Wi-Fi
Access to Presidio's internal network is restricted to authorized employees and managed devices only. Guest Wi-Fi is available for visitors and personal devices with limited access to internal resources. The guest network requires registration and expires after 24 hours. Employees hosting guests must register them through the IT Portal and ensure guests do not access confidential information. Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network via ethernet or attempting to bypass network security controls is strictly prohibited.
19. End of Employment Device Return Procedure
Upon termination of employment (voluntary or involuntary), employees must return all company-issued technology assets within 3 business days. This includes laptops, mobile devices, monitors, keyboards, mice, headsets, security tokens, and access badges. IT will schedule a device return appointment and perform data sanitization according to industry standards. Failure to return equipment may result in deductions from final paychecks where legally permitted and may affect reference checks. Former employees' access to all systems is revoked effective on their last day of employment.
20. Technology Training and Awareness Programs
All Presidio employees must complete mandatory IT security awareness training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Training covers topics including phishing recognition, password security, data protection, acceptable use policies, and incident reporting. Department-specific training is provided for roles with access to sensitive systems. Employees who fail to complete required training may have system access restricted until training is completed. The Learning Management System (LMS) tracks completion and sends automated reminders.
